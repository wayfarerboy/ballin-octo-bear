What to look out for in the attic

Rodents, particularly Black Rats, are very agile climbers. They can reach the comfort of the attic with ease, running up and down stucco walls, climbing cable lines or guttering. Black Rats can enter the attic through the smallest hole, making it larger as they gnaw. Squeaking, scratching or rustling noises coming from your attic are often the first sign that you have an infestation. If not controlled, rodents can cause serious damage in the attic, including house fires if they chew through electric cabling and will return year after year to make your attic their home.

Box Bases

Paper and cardboard is soft and easy to chew. It is also perfect to build nests...

Things to look out for

  • Paper or cardboard storage: Mice tend to gnaw on materials softer than their own teeth and will effortlessly chew on paper and cardboard. They are also very adept at getting inside boxes.
  • Fresh evidence: Look out for gnawed boxes or storage containers with teeth marks and scratches. It is easy to distinguish between rat and mice teeth marks. Rodents will chew on most items except for glass and metal.

Target pests for this area:

  • Black Rat (Rattus rattus)
  • House Mouse

Black Rat (Rattus rattus)

Also called Attik Rat

Description
  • 33-51cm long
  • 150-250g
  • Colour: grey, brown or black
  • Long thin tail

House Mouse

Description
  • 11-16cm long
  • 20-30g
  • Colour: grey, brown
  • Food consumption 3-4g per day
